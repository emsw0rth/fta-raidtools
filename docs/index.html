Overview

FTA Raid Tools is a desktop application for managing raids, loot, and attendance for the WoW TBC Anniversary guild From the Ashes. It connects to a Google Sheet for persistent storage and integrates with Raid Helper for attendance tracking.

The application has five main pages, accessible from the navigation bar:

  • Loot History — Import and track loot drops with per-raid deductions
  • Roster — Manage guild members, their specs, and roll modifiers
  • Attendance — Record raid attendance directly from your Raid Helper events
  • Raid — Export current roll modifiers for in-game use
  • Settings — Three sub-tabs: General Settings (Google Sheet + Raid Helper connection), Roll Modifier Settings (min/max bounds), and Raid Settings (per-raid awards and deductions)

For Raiders: Balanced Rolls Explained

This section is for guild members who participate in raids but don't use the FTA Raid Tools app themselves. It explains how Balanced Rolls work in-game, how you gain and lose roll modifier points, and what happens when you win an item.

What are Balanced Rolls?

Every raider has a personal roll modifier — a number between 0.75 and 1.75 that scales your in-game /roll. Officers and master looters maintain it via FTA Raid Tools (this app), and the modifier reaches the raid through two in-game addons developed for the guild:

  • Balanced Rolls — works alongside Gargul. When players /roll for an item, the master looter sees each player's roll multiplied by their modifier.
  • Gargul History — tracks who won what during the raid so officers can apply item-win deductions later.

Example: you /roll and the dice land on 80. If your modifier is 0.9, the master looter sees your roll as 72. If your modifier is 1.2, they see 96. The modifier rewards consistent attendance and balances against players who frequently win loot.

What's my starting modifier?

New members join with a roll modifier of 1.0 — in-game rolls are unchanged from the dice value.

How do I gain points?

  • Raid completion — Attending a raid that runs to completion awards points.
  • Available on bench — Counted the same as attending. If officers ask you to bench, you still get the award.

How do I lose points?

  • Winning items — Each main-spec item you win deducts points. Off-spec wins don't count. There's a per-raid cap so a single big-loot night can't tank your modifier.
  • Unexcused absence — Signing up but not showing, or pulling out of a bench slot last-minute, deducts points.
  • Did not sign up — If you don't sign up at all, a smaller deduction is applied.

What happens when I win an item?

After the raid, the master looter exports the loot history with the in-game addon and an officer imports it into FTA Raid Tools. For each item you won, the import dialog has an OS checkbox — if the item went to your off-spec, ticking that box excludes it from the deduction. Main-spec items are deducted up to the per-raid cap.

Excused vs. unexcused absence

Life happens — vacations, work, family stuff, emergencies. As long as you let the officer team know in good time, your absence is treated as excused and you lose no points.

Skipping a raid without telling anyone — not signing up, not showing up after signing up, or cancelling a bench position late — counts as unexcused absence and a deduction is applied.

Tip: A quick message in Discord is enough. Officers want raids to fill out, not to punish you for having a life.

Per-raid points

We've theorised extensively (with help from Claude and ChatGPT) to land on values that feel fair for each raid. Karazhan has more bosses and is easier than Gruul's Lair, so the per-completion award is smaller. Item-win and absence deductions are the same across raids.

Min/max cap

Roll modifiers are clamped between 0.75 (minimum) and 1.75 (maximum). The floor protects against a string of bad luck driving someone unrecoverably down; the ceiling prevents long-time low-loot raiders from snowballing far above everyone else.

Worked examples

Example 1: New member, lucky in Karazhan

A new member joins the guild with a modifier of 1.0. They attend Karazhan and win 3 items.

  • Raid completion: +0.1
  • Item wins: 3 × −0.15 = −0.45, capped at −0.4
  • New modifier: 1.0 + 0.1 − 0.4 = 0.7
  • Floored to the minimum cap: 0.75

The next week they attend Karazhan again and roll for one item. Their modifier is 0.75, so a /roll of 69 shows up to the master looter as 69 × 0.75 = 51.75. They lose the item to someone else.

  • Raid completion: +0.1
  • No items won.
  • New modifier going into next raid: 0.85

Example 2: Veteran with the upper cap kicking in

A veteran has been raiding consistently for weeks without winning any items. Their modifier is already at 1.7. They attend Karazhan, complete the raid, and don't roll on anything.

  • Raid completion: +0.1
  • 1.7 + 0.1 = 1.8, capped at the maximum 1.75.

The cap means there's no benefit to "saving up" past 1.75 — once you're at the ceiling, your modifier stays there until you win loot or miss raids.

Example 3: Missing a raid — with and without notice

A raider at 1.0 can't make Gruul this week. Two scenarios:

  • They tell officers on the Friday before raid that they have a work conflict. The absence is excused: no deduction, modifier stays at 1.0.
  • They sign up, then ghost. Unexcused absence: −0.25, modifier becomes 0.75.

Settings

The Settings page is organised into three sub-tabs along the top: General Settings, Roll Modifier Settings, and Raid Settings. The General Settings tab opens by default and is what you'll configure first when setting up the app.

Connection (General Settings tab)

  • Google Sheet URL — The full URL of the Google Sheet used for data storage. The app reads from and writes to this sheet.
  • Service Account Key File — A JSON key file for a Google Cloud service account. Click Browse... to select the file on your machine.
Tip: Make sure you share the Google Sheet with the service account email address (found in the key file as client_email) with Editor permissions.

Raid Helper (General Settings tab)

The Raid Helper section lets the app pull events directly from your Discord server's Raid Helper bot, eliminating the need to copy event URLs by hand.

  • Discord Server ID — The numeric ID of your Discord server. Enable Discord's developer mode (Settings → Advanced → Developer Mode), right-click your server name, and choose "Copy Server ID".
  • API Key — A per-server token issued by the Raid Helper bot. Run /apikey in any channel of your Discord server to view it. The slash command requires the Administrator or Manage Server permission. If the key ever leaks, run /apikey again to refresh it.
  • Earliest event date to load — A date filter applied when fetching events. Events before this date are excluded so the picker stays focused on relevant raids. Defaults to 2026-02-04.
Tip: The API key is sensitive — treat it like a password. It's stored only in your local app config, never in the Google Sheet.

Click Save on the General Settings tab to persist all of the above.

Roll Modifier Settings

The Roll Modifier Settings sub-tab on the Settings page sets global bounds for every player's roll modifier. These cap how high awards can push a value and how low deductions can pull it.

  • Minimum rollModifier — The lowest a player's roll modifier can go (e.g. 0). Deductions will not reduce a modifier below this value.
  • Maximum rollModifier — The highest a player's roll modifier can reach (e.g. 10). Awards will not increase a modifier above this value.

This tab has its own Save button that writes the values to the settings tab of your Google Sheet.

Raid Settings

The Raid Settings sub-tab on the Settings page lets you configure per-raid awards and deductions. Each raid (e.g. Karazhan, Gruul's Lair) can have its own values, and the chosen raid drives the points calculations during attendance and loot import.

Toolbar Actions

  • Load from Sheet — Loads raid settings from the raidsettings tab of your Google Sheet
  • Save to Sheet — Saves current raid settings back to the sheet
  • Add new Raid — Opens a dialog to create a new raid configuration
  • Delete Selected — Removes selected raid configurations

Attendance

The Attendance page lets you record raid attendance by pulling sign-up data from Raid Helper events. It automatically matches event sign-ups to roster members and applies roll modifier awards and deductions.

Creating an Attendance Entry

Click New Entry to open the event picker.

  1. Select the Raid from the dropdown (this determines which raid settings drive awards and deductions).
  2. Browse the list of past events fetched directly from your Raid Helper server. Use the search field at the top to filter by event name (case-insensitive substring match), and toggle Show already-imported to reveal events you've already processed (they're hidden by default).
  3. Click an event row to load its data into the attendance form.
Tip: The picker only shows events on or after the Earliest event date configured in Settings → General Settings. Older events are filtered out before they reach you.

Reviewing Attendance

The attendance form opens in a modal with a scrollable body and a pinned Confirm & Award footer. Each attendee row shows class, spec, role, award points, the matched roster member, and an attended checkbox.

  • Award — The points to be added to the player's roll modifier (from the raid's "Award for Completion" setting).
  • Award To — Which roster member the points are awarded to (auto-matched by Raid-Helper name, falling back to character name).
  • Attended — Checkbox to confirm the player actually attended. Uncheck to exclude them from receiving an award.

Unknown Players

Sign-ups whose name doesn't match any roster member are pulled into a dedicated Unknown Players section at the very top of the form — this is where the rows that need your attention sit. For each unknown sign-up, pick a roster member from the Credit to dropdown to award them the points, or leave the default "(don't credit)" for a pug or a new member you don't want to credit yet.

  • If you pick a roster member, that person is awarded as if the sign-up had matched directly.
  • If you leave "(don't credit)", the sign-up is silently skipped on confirm — no warning, no roster change.

Absences and Sign-Up Tracking

Below the attendees list, the form shows two additional sections:

  • Absences — Roster members who signed up as Absent. Reference list only.
  • Did not sign up — Roster members who didn't sign up for the event at all. A deduction (configured per-raid) can be applied to their roll modifier.

Review the deductions, check the Apply checkbox for any "did not sign up" entries you want to penalise, then click Confirm & Award to apply all awards and deductions. The footer shows progress while the roster, attendance, and import-history sheets save in parallel; if anything fails, the error appears inline in the footer instead of being hidden.

Previous Events

The list under the attendance toolbar shows every event you've recorded, sorted newest-first and capped at 45vh with a scrollbar. Each row has a × button that deletes the entry from both the local store and the attendance tab of the Google Sheet (with confirmation).

Tip: Deleting a previous event does not remove it from the rh-import-history tab, so the picker still treats it as imported. Toggle Show already-imported if you want to re-import it.

Loot History

The Loot History page tracks all loot drops across raids. Loot data is imported via CSV and automatically applies item win deductions to players' roll modifiers based on the selected raid's settings.

Toolbar Actions

  • Load from Sheet — Pulls loot history from Google Sheets
  • Save to Sheet — Pushes loot history to Google Sheets
  • Import Data — Opens the CSV import dialog
  • Add New — Manually add a single loot entry
  • Delete Selected — Removes selected loot entries

Importing Loot Data

Click Import Data to open the import dialog.

First, select the Raid this loot data belongs to. The raid selection determines which deduction values are applied.

Paste your CSV loot data into the text area. The data must include a header row with the following columns:

Date,Player,Item,ItemID 2026-04-01 20:08:42,Mehndi,Adornment of Stolen Souls,28762 2026-04-01 19:20:05,Mehndi,Lionheart Helm,12640

Verifying the Import

After pasting and clicking Import, a verification dialog appears showing all parsed entries. Here you can mark items as Off-Spec (OS) — off-spec items do not count toward roll modifier deductions.

Check the OS? checkbox for any off-spec items, then click Verify and import to finalize. The item win deductions from the selected raid's settings will be applied to each player's roll modifier, up to the configured maximum deduction cap.

Raid

The Raid page is a single-purpose tool for exporting current roll modifiers from a specific raid sign-up. The output is JSON copied to your clipboard, ready to paste into your in-game tool of choice.

Export Roll Modifiers

  1. Click Export Roll Modifiers on the Raid page.
  2. Pick a past Raid Helper event from the same picker used for attendance.
  3. For each non-Absent sign-up the app looks up the matching roster member and lists their current roll modifier alongside the sign-up name.
  4. Click Export to clipboard to copy the result as JSON.

This page is read-only — nothing is written back to the sheet, so importing here doesn't get recorded in rh-import-history.

Raid Night Workflow

Here's a typical workflow for processing a raid night:

  1. Before the raid — Ensure your Roster and Raid Settings are loaded from the sheet and up to date.
  2. During the raid — Go to the Raid page and use Export Roll Modifiers with the current event's sign-ups to copy roll modifiers into your in-game roll tool.
  3. After the raid — Go to the Attendance page, click New Entry, pick the event from the picker, handle any unknown players in the top section, then Confirm & Award.
  4. Import loot — Go to Loot History, import the loot CSV from the raid, verify off-spec items, and confirm.
  5. Save everything — Save the Loot History to the sheet (Roster and Attendance saves happen automatically when you confirm an attendance entry).

How Roll Modifiers Work

Roll modifiers are the core mechanic of the balanced loot system. Each player has a roll modifier value that goes up when they attend raids and goes down when they win items or miss events.

Increases

  • Raid completion — Each attended raid awards points based on the raid's "Award for Completion" value

Decreases

  • Item wins — Winning a main-spec item deducts points based on the raid's "Item Win Deduction" value, up to the "Items Deduction Max" cap per import
  • Unexcused absence — Signing up but not attending deducts points
  • Did not sign up — Not signing up for an event can result in a deduction if applied by an officer

Boundaries

Roll modifiers are clamped between the Minimum rollModifier and Maximum rollModifier values configured on the Settings page. This prevents values from going too low (punishing inactive players forever) or too high (hoarding points).

Tip: Off-spec item wins do not affect roll modifiers. Mark items as OS during the loot import verification step to exclude them from deductions.
